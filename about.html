Dinosaur Categories

  • Carnivorous Dinosaurs

    Carnivorous dinosaurs, also known as theropods, were meat-eating dinosaurs that preyed on other animals. They had sharp teeth, strong jaws, and some of them were capable of running at high speeds.

  • Herbivorous Dinosaurs

    Herbivorous dinosaurs were plant-eating dinosaurs that fed on vegetation such as leaves, fruits, and seeds. They had specialized teeth and jaws for grinding plant material.

  • Flying Dinosaurs

    Flying dinosaurs, known as pterosaurs, were reptiles that evolved the ability to fly. They had wings made of skin and were adapted for aerial locomotion.

  • Aquatic Dinosaurs

    Aquatic dinosaurs, such as ichthyosaurs and mosasaurs, lived in the water. They had streamlined bodies, flippers, and some of them were capable swimmers.

Dinosaur History

Dinosaurs lived during the Mesozoic Era, which spanned from around 252 million years ago to 66 million years ago. This era is divided into three periods: the Triassic, Jurassic, and Cretaceous.

Dinosaurs were a diverse group of reptiles that varied greatly in size, shape, and behavior. They ranged from small, bird-like dinosaurs to massive, long-necked and long-tailed dinosaurs.
