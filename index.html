Categories of Dinosaurs

Herbivores

Herbivorous dinosaurs were plant-eating creatures that roamed the Earth millions of years ago. They had specialized teeth and beaks to consume various types of vegetation.

Carnivores

Carnivorous dinosaurs were fierce predators that hunted and fed on other dinosaurs or animals. They had sharp teeth, strong jaws, and claws for capturing and tearing apart their prey.

Flying

Flying dinosaurs, known as pterosaurs, were not actual dinosaurs but a group of reptiles that lived alongside dinosaurs. They had wingspans ranging from a few feet to over 30 feet.

Aquatic

Aquatic dinosaurs, such as the mighty mosasaurus and the spinosaurus, adapted to life in water. They were excellent swimmers and had features like streamlined bodies and paddle-like limbs.
